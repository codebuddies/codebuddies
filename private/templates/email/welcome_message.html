Welcome to Codebuddies

What exactly is CodeBuddies?

We learn better together.

We’re a community of independent code learners sharing knowledge & helping each other learn faster. We come from all over the world. Everyone is welcome - no previous experience required.

CodeBuddies is a 100% volunteer-run community & our website www.codebuddies.org is open-sourced on https://github.com/codebuddiesdotorg/codebuddies.

This community thrives thanks to the generosity of our volunteers: Slack admin moderators, GitHub code contributors, website community mentors, and generous donations via Open Collective https://opencollective.com/codebuddies.

Let's Get Started

  1. Don't be shy! Introduce yourself in the #introduce-yourself Slack channel and welcome fellow new members. The more we know about each other, the better we can connect and collaborate. :)
  2. Browse all our public Slack channels by clicking on the “CHANNELS” link in the sidebar.
  3. Make sure to read our CodeBuddies Slack Etiquette for General Members
  4. Log into our website http://codebuddies.org using your new CodeBuddies Slack account & take a look around - or join a hangout.
  5. Schedule a hangout yourself! (You don’t need to be an expert to collaborate.) Simply click on the [Schedule a Hangout] button on the website & fill in the information. All hangouts get automatically posted back to our Slack #announcements channel.
  6. Interested in going deep on topics like functional javascript or that special ruby book or blockchain? Start a study group on codebuddies.org/study-groups! All study groups created on the website get automatically posted back to our Slack #announcements channel. Invite folks on Slack to join in so that you can motivate each other to master your learning goal.

If you have any questions about the community or the project or want to talk to an admin, feel free to ask about it in the #codebuddies-meta Slack channel. That’s also where discussions related to our community happen.

Happy learning!
